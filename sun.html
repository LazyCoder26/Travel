Sun Temple, Konark

The Sun Temple in Konark, Odisha, is a breathtaking architectural wonder dedicated to the sun god Surya. Built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty, the temple is designed in the shape of a gigantic chariot with intricate stone carvings and sculptures.

The temple complex was originally located on the sea coast, and its unique design allowed the first rays of the sun to pass through the main entrance and illuminate the main sanctum at dawn. The Sun Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most renowned landmarks in India.

The temple's architecture represents the magnificence of ancient Indian art and engineering. The detailed carvings on the walls depict scenes from mythology, animals, celestial beings, and daily life during the medieval period. The grand chariot is pulled by 24 elaborately carved stone wheels, each representing a different hour of the day.
