Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic white marble mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away during childbirth. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the most beautiful and elegant buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The architecture of the Taj Mahal is a mesmerizing blend of Islamic, Persian, and Indian styles. The stunning white marble exterior is adorned with intricate carvings, calligraphy, and precious gemstones. The Taj Mahal is particularly renowned for its symmetrical design and perfect proportions. It is said to change colors depending on the time of day, glowing pinkish in the morning, dazzling white during the day, and golden in the moonlight. The Taj Mahal is a symbol of eternal love and a masterpiece of architectural brilliance.
